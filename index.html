Chilli Chicken

Spicy, tangy, and flavorful Indo-Chinese style chicken dish.

Ingredients:

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into cubes
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp chili sauce
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 capsicum, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 2-3 tbsp oil for frying
  • Salt to taste

Procedure:

  1. Marinate chicken with soy sauce, chili sauce, ginger-garlic paste, cornflour, flour, egg, and salt for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the chicken pieces until golden brown. Drain excess oil.
  3. In another pan, saut√© onions, capsicum, and green chilies for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add fried chicken, extra chili sauce, soy sauce, and salt. Toss everything together for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Serve hot as a starter or with fried rice/noodles.

Cheesecake

Rich, creamy, and smooth cheesecake with a buttery biscuit base.

Ingredients:

  • 200g digestive biscuits
  • 100g butter, melted
  • 300g cream cheese
  • 200ml heavy cream
  • 100g sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Optional: fruit topping or chocolate sauce

Procedure:

  1. Crush biscuits and mix with melted butter. Press into a greased pan to form the base.
  2. Beat cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla until smooth.
  3. Whip heavy cream separately until stiff peaks form and fold into cream cheese mixture.
  4. Pour the mixture over the biscuit base and smooth the top.
  5. Refrigerate for 4-6 hours or until set.
  6. Top with fruits or chocolate sauce before serving.

Puliyodarai

Tamarind-flavored rice, spiced and tempered the traditional South Indian way.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 1 lemon-sized tamarind ball
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp peanuts
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp chana dal
  • 1 tsp urad dal
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida
  • Curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Puliyodarai spice powder (optional, for extra flavor)

Procedure:

  1. Soak tamarind in warm water and extract thick juice.
  2. Heat oil, fry peanuts until golden, remove and set aside.
  3. In the same oil, temper mustard, dals, chilies, curry leaves, and asafoetida.
  4. Add tamarind extract, salt, and cook until thick.
  5. Mix the tamarind paste into cooked rice and add fried peanuts.
  6. Rest for 30 minutes before serving for best flavor.

Kalan (Mushroom Curry)

A Kerala-style yogurt-based curry made with mushrooms and coconut.

Ingredients:

  • 200g mushrooms (sliced)
  • 1 cup yogurt (sour curd)
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • Salt as needed
  • 2 tsp coconut oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • Curry leaves
  • 2 dried red chilies

Procedure:

  1. Cook mushrooms with turmeric, salt, and little water until soft.
  2. Grind coconut, cumin, and green chilies into a fine paste.
  3. Add paste and yogurt to the mushrooms. Simmer gently (do not boil after yogurt is added).
  4. Prepare tempering with coconut oil, mustard, red chilies, and curry leaves.
  5. Pour over curry and serve with rice.

Strawberry Pudding

Light, creamy, and fruity strawberry pudding topped with fresh strawberries.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • 1/2 cup strawberry puree
  • 1 tsp vanilla essence
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • Chopped strawberries (for garnish)

Procedure:

  1. Mix cornflour in 1/4 cup milk and keep aside.
  2. Boil remaining milk with sugar, then stir in cornflour mixture.
  3. Cook until thick, add vanilla and strawberry puree.
  4. Cool slightly, fold in whipped cream.
  5. Pour into bowls, refrigerate for 2-3 hours.
  6. Garnish with fresh strawberries before serving.

Red Velvet Cake

  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp vinegar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp red food coloring
  • Cream cheese frosting (for layering and topping)

Procedure:

  4. Add flour and buttermilk alternately, mixing gently.
  5. Combine baking soda with vinegar, fold into the batter.
  6. Bake 30‚Äì35 mins, cool completely.

Momos

Steamed dumplings stuffed with flavorful vegetables or chicken, served with spicy chutney.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup grated carrot
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 tsp ginger (grated)
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • (Optional) 1 cup minced chicken for chicken momos

Procedure:

  1. Knead flour with water and a pinch of salt to make a smooth dough. Rest 30 mins.
  2. Heat oil, saut√© garlic, ginger, onion, and veggies. Add soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Cook lightly.
  3. Roll dough into small discs, place stuffing in the center, fold and pleat into dumplings.
  4. Steam momos in a steamer for 10‚Äì12 minutes until soft and cooked.
  5. Serve hot with spicy tomato chutney.
